Alleviate’22

"Alleviate '22" was a collaborative initiative by the Red Cross Society of Royal College and the United Nations Club of Visakha Vidyalaya, standing as a pinnacle fundraising endeavor for the year. This event was a music carnival containing many events, all of which are arranged by the organising committee.

The core mission behind this project is to harness the power of collective action for the betterment of society and beyond. Through Alleviate '22, we aim to generate vital funds to catalyze an array of community service projects that resonate with the fundamental principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement.

Alleviate'22 encompasses a wide spectrum of objectives, all designed to uplift and transform lives. We are committed to enhancing the well-being, safety, and overall quality of life for individuals and communities. Furthermore, we are dedicated to fostering the sustainability of economic and social development, preserving the precious ecosystems that house our wildlife and wilderness, and fortifying the bedrock of human dignity.

Alleviate '22 was not just a carnival; it's a beacon of hope and progress, where every contribution brings us one step closer to creating a brighter, more inclusive future for all. To make a profound impact on the world around us.

Infusion '23

Infusion was an Initiative first chartered by the Red Cross Society in 2020 where children from the Vajira Sri Development Centre were given a day full of fun activities and donations were handed over to the organization at the end of the day. This project was organized by Naqeeb Mohamed and Nafees Salley from the Grade 09 batch at that time, and this year also this project aims to make a positive impact on the less fortunate in Colombo by donating food parcels and providing community service.

The first phase of the project involves the distribution of food parcels to those in need. We recognize that many individuals in Colombo are struggling to make ends meet, particularly in the wake of the Economic Crisis. As such, we are committed to providing food to help alleviate some of the financial burden faced by these individuals and to spread the festive cheer of the Sinhalese and Tamil New Year.

The second phase of the project involves community service. We believe that giving back to the community is a fundamental part of being a responsible citizen, we will be donating necessary dry rations and stationery equipments to a children's home.

Through Infusion, we hope to make a positive impact on the lives of those in need, while also fostering a sense of social responsibility and community involvement among our members. We believe that by coming together and working towards a common goal, we can make a real difference in our community.

44th Annual Blood Donation Campaign

The 44th Annual Blood Donation Campaign, organized by the Royal College Red Cross Society, is another milestone in the club's tradition since 1979. Serving as a testament to our commitment to humanitarian causes, this fund-raising project transcends the boundaries of our school, extending its impact to the national blood bank or Sri Lanka and, consequently, benefiting the entire nation.

Building on the success of the 43rd installment, where we upheld our longstanding dedication to this noble cause, the 44th Annual Blood Donation Campaign has proven to be even more remarkable, achieving record-breaking milestones. This year's campaign witnessed the Royal College Red Cross Society orchestrating 6 Blood Donation camps across diverse venues in Sri Lanka. This initiative aimed to maximize donor participation, contributing significantly to the augmentation of our life-saving efforts.

In our pursuit of excellence, we have not only upheld the values instilled by the Red Cross but have surpassed expectations, demonstrating our unwavering commitment to the well-being of our fellow citizens. The 44th Annual Blood Donation Campaign stands as a beacon of unity and compassion, embodying the collective strength of our community in making a positive impact on the healthcare landscape of our nation.

The Royal College Red Cross Society remains steadfast in its mission to champion humanitarian causes, and the success of the 44th Annual Blood Donation Campaign serves as a testament to our unwavering commitment to making a meaningful difference in the lives of those in need.

Vision’23

Vision'23 was an eye donation and eye care project organized by the Royal College Red Cross Society in association with the National Eye Hospital of Sri Lanka for the primary aim of encouraging and mobilizing a large number of registered eye donors for the National Eye Bank of Sri Lanka. By doing so, the project contributed significantly to the improvement of healthcare and played a pivotal role in the battle against blindness in the nation.

This project was dedicated to the cause of eye donation and enhancing eye care services in the country. With a rich history dating back to 1979, the Royal College Red Cross Society had the distinction of being the first school in Sri Lanka to launch an eye donation program, highlighting its commitment to community welfare. Vision'23 was aligned with the core values of the Royal College Red Cross Society, which were rooted in the fundamental principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement. Through this initiative, we strived to extend our reach within and beyond our school, making a meaningful impact on the well-being of our community.

First Aid Unit

The Royal College First Aid Initiative is a testament to the Royal College Red Cross Society's unwavering dedication to the safety and well-being of our school community. Since its inception, this project has become an integral part of our identity. Over the years, our initiative has tirelessly championed the cause of first aid preparedness and response, and this year marks an exciting milestone in our journey.

With a legacy dating back to its establishment in 1978, the Royal College Red Cross Society's First Aid Unit Initiative has consistently adhered to the principles of delivering swift and effective first aid assistance. As we embark on the next phase of this endeavor, we take a moment to reflect on the profound impact we've made and eagerly anticipate the promising opportunities that lie ahead.
